About The Guide

As a young adult moving from a laid-back high school schedule to a much busier college life, I've had to get very serious about prioritizing how I spend my time. Much of which has, in the past, been spent pursuing a plethora of hobbies, from fly fishing to fencing, archery to Brazillian jiu jitsu, and more. While thinking about how I can streamline my life in order to dedicate sufficient time to the things I care most about, I decided that it would be wise to narrow down my hobbies to the ones that I enjoy the most. Around that same time, I was reading Scott Galloway's The Algebra of Wealth and came across his idea of being strategic while choosing your hobbies based on a variety of factors. I've been going through that process for myself and I'm now a firm believer that everyone should make a strategic effort to choose their passions. I've created this hobby guide to help people with that process.

How the Guide Works

I've broken this guide down into 3 steps.

  1. Understand the goal:
    • Why should you audit your hobbies?
    • When should you audit your hobbies?
    • What criteria should your hobby stack fulfill? Create your criteria.
  2. Compare your current hobbies to your criteria:
    • Do multiple hobbies fulfill that same criteria?
    • Are there any hobbies that can't fit into your criteria?
    • Determine which hobbies will be kept and which you will say goodbye to.
    • Determine which gaps exist in your hobby stack.
  3. Fill gaps in hobby stack:
    • Skim through relevant hobby catalogue sections, making note of hobbies that interest you.
    • Choose which hobbies you will add to complete your hobby stack.
